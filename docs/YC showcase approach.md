# YC showcase approach

## 1. The Strategy: Move "Upstream"

YC usually dislikes B2C bias checkers because they are hard to monetize (the people who need them most won't use them). To make this big, think about who *loses money* when news is biased or fake:

- **The Enterprise Play (B2B):** Hedge funds or commodity traders who need "clean" sentiment data to make bets. If a Sri Lankan news outlet reports a strike at a port, is it a real event or political posturing? Your tool provides the "Truth Score."
- **The API Play:** Building the "Information Integrity Layer" for other AI agents. As more people use AI to browse the web, those AI companies (like Perplexity or OpenAI) need a way to filter out hallucinated or heavily skewed source material.
- **The Brand Safety Play:** Helping global advertisers ensure their ads aren't running next to inflammatory, biased "rage-bait" in emerging markets.

## 2. Your "Earned Insight"

Since you are in Sri Lanka, you have a unique vantage point.

> **The Pitch:** "Western LLMs are trained on Western media standards. They fail to catch the specific linguistic nuances and political 'dog whistles' used in South Asian media. We’ve built a specialized engine that identifies framing bias in non-Western markets where the next billion internet users are coming online."
> 

## 3. Technical Execution

Since you’ve already got a "half-baked" version, here’s how to spice it up for the demo:

- **Beyond Sentiment:** Don't just do "Positive/Negative." Track **Omission** (what did this outlet *not* report that others did?) and **Framing** (did they use "protester" or "rioter"?).
- **The "Bias Vector":** Use your background in high-velocity engineering to create a real-time "heat map" of news. If three outlets report the same event, show the mathematical delta between their versions.
- **Validation:** Use your "RSS Bias Checker" logic to show a back-test. "On [Date], our tool flagged this report as 80% biased; three days later, it was retracted."
